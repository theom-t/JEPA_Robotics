# V2.5 Plan: Multi-View Data Augmentation

### 2. Depth Projection + AI Inpainting (The Robust Robotics Method)

For continuous robot trajectories where physical accuracy is critical, the industry standard is to use a hybrid 3D geometry + AI approach:

*   **Step 1:** Run a state-of-the-art monocular depth estimation model like Depth Anything V2 on your dataset images to extract a highly accurate depth map.
*   **Step 2:** Use the depth map to project the 2D image into a 3D Point Cloud.
*   **Step 3:** Move your virtual camera 15 degrees to the left and render a new 2D image. Because you moved the camera, there will be "holes" or black shadows behind the robot arm where the original camera couldn't see.
*   **Step 4:** Pass this image with the holes into a pre-trained inpainting model (like Stable Diffusion Inpainting or LaMa) to seamlessly fill in the background textures.

**Why Method 2 is better for you:** It mathematically guarantees that the robot's kinematics, distances, and trajectory remain perfectly accurate in the new camera view, while the AI is only used to hallucinate the missing background pixels. This allows you to effortlessly multiply your SO100 and BridgeData datasets by 10x with mathematically valid synthetic camera angles!
